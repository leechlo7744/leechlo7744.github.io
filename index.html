Inventory Risk Analysis
Using SQL

SKU-level SQL analysis combining sales, forecast, inventory, and inbound data to surface stockout risk, forecast gaps, and replenishment priorities — connecting order flow and inbound timing to execution decisions.

Panel 01 — Business Question

Which SKUs will stock out before the next inbound shipment arrives?

Four tables joined to compute net available inventory, average weekly demand, and weeks-of-supply per SKU — then cross-referenced against inbound ETAs and vendor lead time risk to flag replenishment gaps before they become stockouts.

Data Sources
products sales_orders inventory_snapshot inbound_shipments
8 SKUs  ·  4 categories
Panel 02 — SQL Logic & Risk Flag Criteria

Net Available strips committed and QA-hold before WOS is calculated. Risk is flagged when supply runs out before the next vendor ETA.

Core Formulas
Net Available
= on_hand − committed
  − qa_hold

WOS = net_available
      ÷ avg_weekly_demand

Forecast Gap
= units_sold − forecast_qty
Risk Flag Triggers
Critical

WOS < 2 weeks or inbound ETA exceeds lead time window

Watch

Positive forecast gap (sold > forecast) signals underplanning

Overstock

Negative forecast gap with high on-hand → holding cost risk

Panel 03 — Key Findings & Recommendations

All SKUs showed sub-1 week supply coverage, with the highest risk concentrated in pending inbound items and longer vendor lead times.

As of the Dec 31 snapshot, every SKU shows WOS under 1 week. Velvet Tint (Makeup) is most urgent at WOS 0.05 with a +467-unit forecast gap — demand consistently outpaced plan. Glow Serum and Sun Base also show positive forecast gaps, signaling a systematic underforecast in Skincare.

Apparel runs the opposite risk: Linen Shirt and Slim Jeans show negative gaps (−196 to −201 units) — forecast exceeded actual demand, increasing overstock exposure. Recommendation: expedite Night Cream and Daily Tote (both Pending), trigger immediate replenishment for Velvet Tint, and revisit Apparel forecast inputs before the next planning cycle.

Panel 02 — Inventory Risk Assessment

Two simultaneous risks: Skincare & Makeup at stockout, Apparel at overstock — both requiring opposite action at the same time.

Stockout Risk

Velvet Tint at 45 units and Silk Scarf at 20 units heading into Q4 peak. Procurement needed to be triggered 2–3 months in advance to absorb the seasonal demand spike.

Overstock Risk

Apparel category carrying 905 excess units, inflating holding costs. Lead times across South Korea (15 days) to Italy (33 days) create a 2× variance that compounds the timing risk.

Panel 03 — Strategic Action Plan

Three recommendations. One goal: protect Q4 revenue and reduce holding cost exposure.

01

Replenish +1,770 units of Skincare & Makeup immediately to prevent lost Q4 sales during peak demand.

02

Implement a 20% safety stock buffer across all categories to absorb vendor lead time variability.

03

Launch targeted Apparel promotions to accelerate sell-through and recover holding costs.
