Case Study · Supply Chain · 2025

Supply Chain &
Inventory Risk Analysis

A data-driven analysis of 2025 demand trends and inventory risks across the fashion & beauty industry. Using Tableau and Excel, this project identifies Q4 stockout risks, maps global lead time complexity, and delivers a strategic replenishment action plan.

Tableau  ·  Excel  ·  PowerPoint  ·  4 Categories  ·  6 Slides

View full case study on desktop
Slide 01 — Overview

Data-Driven Optimization for Supply Chain Resilience

This project applies Tableau and Excel-based analytics to address a core operational challenge in the fashion & beauty industry: balancing volatile demand with inventory precision. The goal — minimize stockouts, reduce overstock, and build a replicable planning framework.

Slide 02 — Project Overview & Objectives

Fast-moving demand requires faster visibility.

In the fashion & beauty space, stock imbalances happen fast. The objective was clear: analyze 2025 sales trends and vendor lead times to minimize stockouts and optimize inventory turnover — delivering demand forecasting, risk assessment, and a replenishment strategy as key outputs.

Slide 03 — Demand Trend Analysis

Makeup surged +1,200 units in Q4 — and no one saw it coming.

The Tableau monthly sales chart revealed a sharp Q4 spike in the Makeup category, driven by holiday seasonal demand. The takeaway: procurement needed to be triggered 2–3 months in advance to absorb this peak without risking an out-of-stock event.

Slide 04 — Supply Chain & Lead Time Complexity

Lead times vary by 2× across vendor regions — that gap is the risk.

Vendors span South Korea, China, Vietnam, Italy, and France. South Korea ships in ~15 days; Vietnam and Italy require 28–33 days. By integrating lead time data into the Reorder Point formula — ROP = (Avg Daily Usage × Lead Time) + Safety Stock — the analysis pinpointed exactly when to reorder per SKU.

Slide 05 — Inventory Risk Assessment

Two risks identified: a stockout and an overstock, simultaneously.

Skincare & Makeup faced a critical stockout alert — with Velvet Tint down to 45 units and Silk Scarf at 20 units heading into peak. At the same time, the Apparel category held 905 excess units, inflating holding costs. Both required opposite but equally urgent actions.

Slide 06 — Strategic Action Plan

Three recommendations. One goal: protect revenue and reduce waste.

Recommendation 1: Immediately replenish +1,770 units of Skincare & Makeup to prevent lost Q4 sales. Recommendation 2: Implement a 20% Safety Stock policy to buffer against lead time variability. Recommendation 3: Launch targeted Apparel promotions to accelerate sell-through and recover holding costs.
